THE STORY OF ATLANTIS
01 Origins & Challenge

A town built for exclusion.
A community that endured.

Atlantis was established in the 1970s under apartheid's Group Areas Act — an industrial township deliberately placed far from Cape Town's economic centre, designed to house Coloured workers at a distance from the city they built. When the manufacturing sector collapsed in the 1980s, the town was left behind with an unemployment rate that peaked above 80 percent.

What followed was decades of poverty, social instability, and a profound sense of abandonment by the systems that created the town. But the community remained. The work ethic, the manufacturing knowledge, and the industrial infrastructure did not disappear — they waited.

80% Peak unemployment rate in Atlantis before SEZ designation — the injustice the zone was built to reverse.
02 The Turning Point

The designation that changed everything.

In 2015, the South African government recognised what others had missed: Atlantis already had the bones of a world-class industrial node. Proximity to the Port of Cape Town. Access to the N7 corridor. Proximity to Koeberg. A community with deep manufacturing heritage. A fully serviced industrial area ready for investment.

The SEZ designation unlocked the framework for a deliberate industrial transition — one focused specifically on green technology, renewable energy manufacturing, and the clean industries that would define the next century of global manufacturing. The first factories broke ground. The first workers were hired. The plan became reality.

2015 Year of SEZ designation — the moment a plan became a commitment backed by law, infrastructure, and investment.
03 The Vision

Africa's leading green
manufacturing destination.

The ambition is not modest. Atlantis is positioned to demonstrate that the transition away from fossil fuels can be an instrument of genuine social equity — that the green economy can create jobs, build local skills, grow local businesses, and deliver shared prosperity in communities that industrial capitalism left behind.

Atlantis Capital is the digital infrastructure that makes this ecosystem visible, measurable, and connected. It links job seekers to employers, SMMEs to supply chains, training institutions to labour market signals, and investors to real-time impact data. The Twin Transition — digital and green simultaneously — is not a slogan. It is the operating model.

12,400 Projected direct and indirect jobs at full zone build-out — each one representing a family's economic future.

THE INVESTMENT OPPORTUNITY

The Case Is Clear.
The Timing Is Now.

The Atlantis SEZ offers a rare convergence of infrastructure readiness, policy support, skilled labour availability, and global demand tailwinds for green manufacturing.

Strategic Location

50 km from Port of Cape Town. Direct N7 highway access. Rail connection available. Proximity to Koeberg and the Western Cape's renewable energy corridor.

Infrastructure Ready

Fully serviced development-ready land. Power, water, fibre, and roads already in place. No greenfield development risk — plug in and operate.

Skilled Workforce

A community with deep manufacturing heritage and a rapidly growing pipeline of trained green economy workers through the Atlantis Capital platform.

Regulatory Support

Dedicated SEZ tax incentives, customs relief, and the active support of national, provincial, and local government aligned behind a single development objective.

Market Tailwinds

African green energy investment is accelerating. RE capacity additions are forecast to triple by 2030. Atlantis is positioned at the supply end of this demand curve.

Proven Track Record

47 companies established. R8.2bn committed. 3,840 jobs created. The zone is not a concept — it is a functioning, growing industrial ecosystem.
